# Chapter 1: Wireless and Mobile Security Foundations

> The domain first: how wireless and mobile systems are attacked, and where their trust
> boundaries lie — before any AI enters the picture.

**ECE 669 · [Course README](../../README.md) · [Syllabus](../../SYLLABUS.md)**

> 🚧 **Status:** landing page + outline. Synthetic capture-analysis lab coming.

## Topics

- **Wireless threat models** — the shared, broadcast medium and why it changes the attacker's
  reach.
- **Physical-layer attack surface** — jamming, spoofing (incl. GPS), eavesdropping, and RF
  fingerprinting. *(Treated as attack surface and later as an AI-diagnosable domain — not as
  an information-theoretic secrecy course.)*
- **Identity, authentication, and subscriber privacy** — 5G-AKA, SUPI/SUCI, EAP, device
  identity.
- **Protocol trust boundaries** — what each layer is allowed to assume about its inputs.
- **Wireless control consequences** — power, beam, scheduling, handover: the surfaces later
  chapters show an AI can be manipulated into affecting.

## Lab

**Trust-boundary map** — annotate a **synthetic** capture/log: mark the identities present and
draw the trust boundaries. No real traffic.
